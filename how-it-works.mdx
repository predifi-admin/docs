## What Are Prediction Markets?

Prediction markets (also called "information markets" or "event markets") let you trade on the outcome of future events.

### The Concept

Instead of betting against a bookmaker, you trade shares with other users. Each share represents a claim on a specific outcome:

- **YES shares** pay $1 if the event happens
- **NO shares** pay $1 if the event doesn't happen

The **current price** reflects the market's collective belief about the probability of that outcome.

<Note>
  **Example**: If a YES share for "Will Bitcoin hit $100k in 2025?" trades at $0.65, the market estimates a 65% probability.
</Note>

### Why They're Powerful

Prediction markets have proven to be remarkably accurate because:

1. **Financial Incentives** - Traders profit from being correct, creating strong motivation for accuracy
2. **Collective Intelligence** - Markets aggregate information from many participants
3. **Price Discovery** - Continuous trading reveals real-time probability estimates
4. **Self-Correcting** - Mispriced markets create arbitrage opportunities that push prices toward truth

## How Trading Works

### Basic Trading Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a Market">
    Browse markets on politics, sports, crypto, entertainment, or other categories. Each market has a binary outcome (YES/NO).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Analyze the Odds">
    Review current prices, volume, liquidity, and historical charts. Consider whether the market is correctly priced.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Take a Position">
    If you think YES is underpriced, buy YES shares. If you think NO is underpriced, buy NO shares.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Hold or Exit">
    Hold your position until the market resolves, or sell early to lock in profits or cut losses.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Settlement">
    When the event resolves, winning shares pay out $1.00 per share. Losing shares expire worthless.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Understanding Prices

Prices in prediction markets represent **implied probability**:

| Price | Implied Probability | Meaning |
|-------|---------------------|---------|
| $0.90 | 90% | Market thinks outcome is very likely |
| $0.65 | 65% | Market thinks outcome is somewhat likely |
| $0.50 | 50% | Market is uncertain (50/50) |
| $0.35 | 35% | Market thinks outcome is somewhat unlikely |
| $0.10 | 10% | Market thinks outcome is very unlikely |

<Tip>
  **Trading Strategy**: If you believe the true probability is higher than the market price, buy YES. If you think it's lower, buy NO.
</Tip>

## Market Resolution

### How Markets Resolve

When the event concludes, markets must be **resolved** to determine winners:

1. **Outcome Determination** - The real-world outcome is observed
2. **Oracle Verification** - Decentralized oracles provide on-chain proof
3. **Smart Contract Settlement** - Winners can claim their payouts
4. **Payout Distribution** - $1.00 per winning share sent to claimants

## Risk Management

### Understanding Risk

Prediction market trading involves risk:

- **Market Risk** - Your prediction may be wrong
- **Liquidity Risk** - You may not be able to exit at desired prices
- **Event Risk** - Unexpected developments can move markets
- **Smart Contract Risk** - Technical vulnerabilities (mitigated through audits)
